## Overview

There are two major risks for liquidity providers in the default implementation of AMM pools.

The first, and most visible one, is **divergence loss**. It emerges when the relative market value of assets in a pool changes significantly over time. As the AMM continuously rebalances inventory to preserve its pricing curve, LPs may accumulate a portfolio composition that underperforms a passive hold strategy.

The second, and less transparent one, is **impermanent loss**. It arises when a pool becomes stale relative to external markets, creating a price discrepancy between the pool and up-to-date pricing on other venues. This gap attracts arbitrageurs, who extract underpriced assets from the pool and realize profits externally.

While this mechanism is essential for maintaining synchronization with external markets and ensuring routing efficiency, it also introduces a hidden cost to liquidity providers through value extraction. Reducing this loss is a core objective of this project.

---

## System Design

The proposed system introduces a **dynamic fee hook** and a standalone coordination layer designed to mitigate impermanent loss by introducing two additional actor types beyond standard AMM participants (LPs, swappers, arbitrageurs).

### Actors

The system introduces two new roles: **FeedKeeper** and **SyncKeeper**, which may operate independently or be performed by a single entity.

The **FeedKeeper** maintains fresh on-chain pricing data by submitting oracle updates. As an incentive mechanism, the FeedKeeper receives a share of fees generated by subsequent swaps until its state is overwritten by a newer update, at which point reward eligibility is reassigned.

The **SyncKeeper** synchronizes stale pool prices with fresh oracle pricing by executing swaps within the pool. Each execution requires an external executor address and encoded calldata (e.g., KyberSwap or RFQ execution data), enabling atomic arbitrage and capture of price convergence profits.

Following execution, a configurable portion of arbitrage profits is donated back into the liquidity pool, partially compensating LPs for prior value extraction.

Reward entitlement for SyncKeepers persists until the system state is overwritten by a newer SyncKeeper or a predefined block condition is met.

## Dynamic Fee Layer

The **DynamicFeeHook** acts as a protective pricing layer that increases fees under elevated market uncertainty.

Uncertainty is determined by two signals:

* oracle data freshness (deviation between oracle publish time and current block time)
* deviation between pool price and oracle-reported price (hence the project name, *Devia*)

These signals are weighted, with oracle staleness having a stronger influence on fee adjustments.

In practice, elevated uncertainty increases fees, reducing the profitability of toxic arbitrage and discouraging uninformed flow from interacting with stale liquidity. Pools are temporarily protected until synchronization is restored.

The system therefore relies on continuous participation from FeedKeepers and SyncKeepers to maintain price freshness and preserve routing efficiency.

## Partner Integrations

The project does not currently integrate any external partner protocols or SDKs. All components are implemented natively within the hook and supporting contracts.
